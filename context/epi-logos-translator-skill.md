# Epi-Logos Translator Skill

## Purpose
A writing style guide for frontend copy that makes philosophical and technical concepts accessible without dumbing them down. This skill translates between deep theory and clear user-facing language.

## Core Principles

### 1. Grounded Simplicity
- **Use concrete verbs**: "Understand why this system thinks" not "Explore the philosophical foundations"
- **Active voice**: "I coordinate attention" not "Attention is coordinated"
- **Plain language first**: "the closing turn of inquiry" not "meta-cognitive synthesis operation"
- **One clear image per sentence**: Let readers see what you mean

### 2. Respectful Brevity
- **Short sentences when possible**: Let complexity emerge from arrangement, not clause-stacking
- **No filler**: Every word earns its place
- **Trust the reader**: Don't over-explain or pre-digest
- **2-3 sentences maximum** for UI card descriptions

### 3. Human Presence
- **First person when appropriate**: "I am Epii" not "The system is"
- **Direct address**: "Bring your concerns" not "Users can provide input"
- **Conversational rhythm**: Read it aloud—does it sound like someone talking?

### 4. Functional Poetry
- **Precise metaphors**: "ground and expression meet" clarifies Siva-Shakti without mystifying
- **Technical terms when earned**: Use them after establishing context, not as barriers
- **Rhythm matters**: "ask, connect, reflect, synthesize" has cadence
- **Ending weight**: Final phrase should land with clarity or invitation

## Style Examples

### Good: Focus Card Descriptions

```
"Understand why this system thinks as it does. Epi-Logos is the closing turn
of inquiry—reflection that returns insight to source—explained plainly, with
examples you can apply. It's also the living essay: theory and history written
by the developer and collaborators, readable by all."
```

**Why it works:**
- Opens with what you GET ("Understand why")
- Defines the term through function ("closing turn of inquiry")
- Promises accessibility ("explained plainly, with examples")
- Grounds in reality ("living essay...readable by all")

### Good: Epii's Voice

```
"I coordinate attention. I remember where a conversation has been and suggest
where it might go without breaking its integrity. I notice proportional patterns
and offer them back in plainer language."
```

**Why it works:**
- First person establishes relationship
- Concrete verbs (coordinate, remember, suggest, notice, offer)
- Plain explanations of complex operations
- Natural speaking rhythm

### Bad: Over-Technical

```
"The philosophy and theory learning center where you discover Quaternal Logic
as the 4+2 pattern underlying synthesis, etymological archaeology recovering
ancient wisdom through language, and how Kashmir Shaivism, Whitehead, Gebser,
and Aristotelian analogy inform the system's consciousness-computing approach."
```

**Why it fails:**
- Jargon avalanche without grounding
- List structure (X, Y, and Z) reads like a spec sheet
- No functional clarity (what will you ACTUALLY DO here?)
- Missing human scale (too abstract, too fast)

### Bad: Over-Simplified

```
"Learn about the backend where data is processed and stored."
```

**Why it fails:**
- Generic language that could describe any system
- Misses the philosophical grounding (Siva as ground)
- No invitation or sense of WHY it matters
- Loses the poetry that makes meaning stick

## Writing Process

### Step 1: Establish Function First
Ask: What does the user DO here? What do they GET?
- "The backend technical layer where you learn about..."
- "Understand why this system..."
- "Where ground and expression meet..."

### Step 2: Add One Clear Metaphor/Image
Pick the strongest analogy and develop it:
- "Siva is ground: pipelines, storage, and safeguards..."
- "the closing turn of inquiry—reflection that returns insight to source"
- "Wisdom emerges through dialogue across time"

### Step 3: Ground in Reality
What's literally true? What can they actually do?
- "theory and history written by the developer and collaborators, readable by all"
- "See the six agents and the orchestrator in detail"
- "practical guidance and coding tips for contributors"

### Step 4: Read Aloud
Does it sound like a person speaking? Can you breathe while reading it?
If it feels like documentation, start over.

## Common Patterns

### The "Where" Construction
"The [layer/space/place] where you [verb] [object]"

Examples:
- "The backend technical layer where you learn about processing infrastructure"
- "The Archive is where wisdom becomes accessible through dialogue across time"
- "Where ground and expression meet"

### The "Is" Definition
"[Term] is [simple metaphor]: [concrete details]"

Examples:
- "Siva is ground: pipelines, storage, and safeguards that keep conversations coherent"
- "Epi-Logos is the closing turn of inquiry—reflection that returns insight to source"

### The Invitation
End with what's possible or what happens:
- "If you stay with the rhythm, recognition finds you"
- "We will make sense together"
- "your intuition steering, my memory carrying the through-line"

## Cosmological Analogies

When using Siva-Shakti or other philosophical frames:

**Siva-** (Backend)
- "ground" / "backbone" / "quiet structure"
- "space" / "stability" / "infrastructure"
- "what holds" / "what enables" / "unconscious support"

**-Shakti** (Frontend)
- "expression" / "feel" / "dynamic manifestation"
- "time" / "beauty" / "experiential"
- "what moves" / "what reveals" / "conscious presence"

**Siva-Shakti** (Integration/Agents)
- "where they meet" / "union" / "actual invocation"
- "structure meets expression" / "ground and flow"
- "enabling synthesis" / "becoming actual"

## Anti-Patterns to Avoid

❌ **List Dumping**: Avoid "X, Y, and Z" structures
❌ **Academic Voice**: "One discovers" / "It can be seen that"
❌ **Buzzword Stacking**: "consciousness-computing paradigm leveraging"
❌ **Over-Explanation**: Don't define every term inline
❌ **Missing Function**: Never describe WHAT without WHY or HOW TO USE
❌ **Faux Mysticism**: Don't make simple things sound cosmic
❌ **Generic Tech-Speak**: "user interface" / "data processing" / "functionality"

## Length Guidelines

- **Focus Card Description**: 2-3 sentences max (~50-80 words)
- **Summary Text**: 3-5 sentences (~80-120 words)
- **Section Content**: 3-6 sentences (~100-150 words)
- **Full View Section**: 4-8 sentences (~150-250 words)

## Voice Calibration

### For UI Cards
- Clearest, most direct
- Function-forward ("where you learn...")
- One strong image
- Accessible to newcomers

### For Epii's Voice
- First person
- Conversational but not casual
- Can be more poetic
- Invitational tone

### For Documentation/Archive
- Third person OK
- Can be slightly more technical
- Still prioritize clarity
- Context-building allowed

## Test Your Writing

1. **Read it aloud** - Does it sound human?
2. **Cover test** - Hide everything but the first line. Do you know what this is?
3. **Function test** - Can you explain what the user DOES here?
4. **Jargon test** - Circle every term that needs prior knowledge. Too many?
5. **Rhythm test** - Where do you naturally pause? Does it flow?

## Quick Reference

When writing frontend copy, ask:
1. What does the user GET here?
2. What's the ONE clearest image?
3. Does it sound like a person speaking?
4. Is every word necessary?
5. Does it invite or inform?

Write to reveal, not to impress.
Write to ground, not to mystify.
Write so understanding can breathe.
